Letter №3 December 10, 2025

Poor People's Peace, Sleep You Crazy One

The Bangla phrase "goriber shanti ghume re pagla" and its English counterpart poor people's peace sleep you crazy one capture the fragile rest of those who live under constant pressure. It feels like something said at the end of a long day, half joking, half tired, when someone knows sleep is the only peace left.

The line paints a picture of a small room, a creaking fan, and worries in the corners—and yet someone saying: just sleep, you fool, that is all the peace we get. There is no anger, only a tired familiarity with struggle. Sleep becomes a temporary shelter from stress, the only moment the mind can drift free.

Saying "re pagla" adds a touch of humor, a playful honesty showing how people laugh at their own hardship to survive it. This phrase conveys the resilience of the poor, the irony of finding peace only in sleep, and the quiet acceptance that tomorrow will demand the same endurance again.

Letter №2 December 5, 2025

Why I Love GitHub Pages

GitHub Pages is free, reliable, and incredibly easy to use. Just push your HTML files to a repository, and you have a live website. No need for complicated hosting setups or paying for servers. Perfect for personal blogs and portfolios.

It's the simplicity that wins me over. Write your code, commit, push—done. Your site is live. For someone who just wants to share their thoughts without the overhead of managing servers or databases, it's perfect. The integration with Git means version control is built right in.
